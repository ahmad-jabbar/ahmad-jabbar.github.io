Ahmad Jabbar
jabbar@stanford.edu

I am a PhD student at Stanford Linguistics, advised by Christopher Potts. I work on natural-language semantics and formal pragmatics. Put simply: using some math and computational tools, I seek to explain how humans compute meaning (in context). My current projects focus on compositionality and discourse structure.

My other interests are in cs theory, nlp, psycholinguistics, and rational analysis. I am affiliated with the Stanford NLP Group and the ALPS lab.

My previous degrees are in logic and philosophy. I have learned a lot from Magdalena Kaufmann, Mitch Green, Bill Lycan, and Lionel Shapiro.
