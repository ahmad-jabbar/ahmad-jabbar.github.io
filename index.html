Ahmad Jabbar

jabbar@stanford.edu

I am a PhD student at Stanford Linguistics. I work on natural language meaning; its useful representations and processing. In my research, I seek to understand things deeply using math; sometimes it's via logic, sometimes statistics. I am affiliated with the Stanford NLP Group and the ALPS lab. My previous degrees are in logic and philosophy. I also house a keen interest in computation theory.

Goings on:

I am writing my first qualifying paper. This research is directed by Christopher Potts (chair), Cleo Condoravdi, and Thomas Icard.

Veda Kanamarlapudi and I recently presented our paper Grounding with particles at SemDial. This paper explores a sui generis grounding move in Hindi-Urdu dialogue.
